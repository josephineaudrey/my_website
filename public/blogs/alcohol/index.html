Alcohol consumption

Where Do People Drink The Most Beer, Wine And Spirits?

While some people might argue that drinking alcohol can provide several health benefits, others believe that you should never drink at all. Regardless of this discussion, where do people drink the most alcohol? And how does this differ by alcohol type. The following visualizations provide answers on regional trends for alcohol preference:

Total Alcohol

# filter for top 25 alcohol consuming countries
top25_alcohol <- drinks %>% 
  top_n(25, total_litres_of_pure_alcohol)  # rank top 25 by alcohol servings

top25_alcohol_plot <- top25_alcohol %>%
  ggplot(aes(
    x = total_litres_of_pure_alcohol,
    y = reorder(country,total_litres_of_pure_alcohol),
    text = paste0(country, ", ", total_litres_of_pure_alcohol, "litres") # order countries by beer servings
  )) +
  geom_col(stat = "identity", width = 0.8, fill = "#c6dbef") + # adapt bars and set a color to beer
  labs(
    x = "Litres of Alcohol", 
    y = "", 
    title = "Top 25 Alcohol Consuming Countries", subtitle = "Belarus, Lithuania, & Andorra", caption = "Source: World Health Organization (GISAH), 2010") + # add labels to axis and title
  NULL

# convert into interactive graph
top25_alcohol_plotly <- ggplotly(top25_alcohol_plot, tooltip = "text")

top25_alcohol_plotly

IMPLICATIONS

  • In the wine consumption rankings, 9 of the top 10 are European countries. Most areas of Europe (especially the low latitudes) are very suitable for growing grapes due to sufficient sunlight and fertile soil. The typical ones are Southern France, Portugal, Andorra, Italy, and Greece. This may develop the peopleâ€™s habit of drinking wine. Compared to wine, the high beer consuming countries are mainly in higher latitudes (north of the Alps), since wheat is widely planted and is an important raw material for beer in these countries. In addition, the beer ranking includes the most continents among other liquors, which may implicate that beer is the most widely accepted and accessible alcohol in the world.
  • In the spirits consumption ranking, six of the top 25 are island countries in the Caribbean and the Pacific, and 11 of the top 25 are East European countries. This conforms to the origins of two very famous spirit types - rum and vodka.
